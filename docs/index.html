#01

Abstract

This project explores the design and construction of a personal language model (PLM) grounded in my own voice, knowledge, beliefs, and emotional patterns. The current phase focuses on structured data collection and identity modeling, establishing a foundation for how an AI system can reflect an individual’s communication style, reasoning, and boundaries. Future phases will introduce fine-tuning and continuous learning, enabling the system to evolve over time. Unlike static digital representations, this approach investigates how a personal AI can adapt alongside its creator—while preserving consistency and avoiding catastrophic forgetting. The long-term goal is a deployed system capable of speaking, reasoning, and interacting as a personalized digital counterpart in real time.
#02

Motivation

Most AI research builds tools for others. This project asks a different question: what does it take to build an AI that is a specific person? Not a simulation of a generic human, but a bounded model of one individual — their knowledge gaps included, their uncertainty preserved, their voice intact.

The secondary research contribution is the continuous learning architecture — a pipeline that ingests daily sentiment data from biometric sensors and fine-tunes the model incrementally, solving the challenge of representing a person who changes over time without catastrophic forgetting of their foundation.
